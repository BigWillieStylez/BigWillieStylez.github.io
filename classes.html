Breath Play Ties: Compression Chokes

Instructor : sassy_masochist & FearlesMasochist

Do you enjoy kink that takes your breath away? We do too! In this hands-on class, we will learn about different types of breath play and discuss risks and risk mitigation associated with chest compression chokes. We will explore a chest compression choke tie and how it can add both physical and emotional intensity to our play; with time to practice on your own. Breath play is edge play, so we ask that all who come to this class take risk mitigation seriously.

Skill Level : Intermediate to Advanced.

Prerequisite : The person tying should feel comfortable setting and maintaining tension, have a basic understanding of anatomy, feel comfortable with upline management, and know a variety of suspension-worthy harnesses and frictions. The person being tied should have an interest in and/or desire to engage with breath compression ties (via compression of the rib cage) and should be able to communicate about their needs and the rope placement.

Materials :Bring a partner, your suspension kit, a notebook and writing implement, and a cutting tool.

Microbondage: All About the Little Things

Instructor : sassy_masochist & FearlesMasochist

Microbondage: both smaller rope and smaller body parts. Learn the intricate art of paying sadistic attention to the little things. In this hands-on class, we will work with different types of thin rope to experiment with tying faces, toes, tongues, and fingers. In addition to playing with new types of rope, we will discuss safety practices and methods of effective nonverbal communication. Bring your creativity, curiosity, and tiny things to this fun class for all experience levels.

Skill Level : All skill levels.

Prerequisite : To participate in this class -- The person tying should have a basic understanding and comfort with tension.- The person being tied should be comfortable with finger and/or face rope and have an ability to communicate about rope placement.

Materials : Bring your creativity, your tying partner and tiny ropes if you have them (tiny rope will also be provided). Please always bring your cutting tool.

The Somersault Suspension

Instructor : Mina & Discolady

This is an intermediate/advanced suspension class. Students will learn a versatile body harness used to transition their bottom through a somersault-like series of positions. These transitions will go from standing, to face-up suspension, to inversion, to face-down suspension and back to standing. We will discuss risk profile and management of this transition sequence, possible points of complication, upline management and sequential flow of the scene. If time allows we will discuss variations.

Skill Level : Intermediate, advance hands on.

Prerequisite : A working knowledge of a chest harness that can be loaded from the front and the back, tying and untying uplines, a risk profile that includes inversions and preferably experience with transitional suspensions.

Materials : Students will need a consenting partner able and willing to be in a dynamic rope suspension that includes an inversion and their own rope kit including preferred hardware used for suspension.

Anatomy Risk Awareness for Shibari

Instructor : Winter Nawa & Dani

Are you new to rope? Do you want a review on the common risks in shibari? Or interested in finding ideal TK/gote placements? Every body is different and finding placements is an important part of mitigating risks in rope bondage. Winter will be reviewing the common anatomy risks involved with shibari. The 2TK and How To Adapt It

Instructor : Winter Nawa & Dani

Do you enjoy the box tie? Want to learn how to tie it? Or want to find a version that works for your partner? Winter will share a basic 2 rope Takate Kote. It is also called Gote Shibari or the Japanese box tie. This class will use the widespread Osada Steve / Kanna / Kinoko stem. Winter will discuss finding ideal wrap placements, arm flexibility options, and kannuki variations for different bodies and situations. They will share typical hand checks and give communication and body awareness advice for bottoms.

Skill Level : beginner to intermediate. Prior knowledge of the TK is not required.

Prerequisite : The prerequisite is a solid single column tie. Knowledge of the cross friction and half moon are helpful but not required as it will be taught during this class.

Materials : 3 full lengths of rope (traditionally 8meter, but other lengths are okay), and an emergency cutting tool.

Kata Ashi - Partial to Full Suspension

Instructor : Winter Nawa & Dani

Have you been tying for a while and interested in learning more about suspension? Are you uncertain if it's for you and your partner and want to dip your toe into the water? Or do you want to review upline management? This is an introduction to suspension class. Winter will share the classical Kata Ashi "one leg" pose and how to transition it to a full suspension. The focus of this class will be a discussion of uplines, how to tie a pulley, and upline management. Winter will use the OS/ Kanna/ Kinoko 2TK but other chest harnesses may be used.

Skill Level : Intermediate to advanced

Prerequisite : The prerequisite is a solid single column tie and 2TK or other suspendable chest harness.

Materials : 7-8 full lengths of rope (traditionally 8meter, but other lengths are okay), and an emergency cutting tool.

Body Bamboo 101

Instructor : Ropeosaur & LilServiceGoblin

What is body bamboo? Why would you consider adding this to your rope repertoire? Let’s discuss all this and more concepts like how using body bamboo can create sadistic levers, spicy predicaments, and unique challenges for our partners. Let us guide you through your first body bamboo rope experience as we teach important safety considerations, a helpful hitch, and attachment methods that will open up a new world of rope experiences for you and your tying partner. After going over some examples of attaching bamboo to the body, we will be providing demonstrations of some fun predicaments to try out while also encouraging attendees to create their own!

‍

Skill Level: Open to all experience levels.

Prerequisite : This is a pre-requisite for the bamboo 201 class. This class is intended to beaccessible to all rope experience levels. Knowledge of hojo cuffs, single and double columns willbe helpful to the successful participation of this class, but not required.

Materials : 2x 4ft bamboo poles. Rope kit including your safety sheers, and a suggested minimum 8 full lengths of rope (~25-30 feet each), and some half lengths. We recommend bringing clothing that can cover the body (particularly the ankles, think thick tall socks) as an option to protect the boney prominences from the bamboo while you practice creating tension while lashing bamboo to the skin.

Body Bamboo 201

Instructor : Ropeosaur & LilServiceGoblin

Let’s build off the information we learned in the 101 class (Note: this is a pre-requisite) to create dynamic suspension bamboo ties. We will discuss how suspending with body bamboo compares to standard suspensions, and how this can increase accessibility for different body types and experiences levels. After learning the icicle hitch, we will examine how to determine which o fthe two hitches we’ve learned (clove & icicle) are chosen for uplines in suspension. We will then be learning 1-2 upper and lower body ties that utilize bamboo as the suspension component. Breaking the ties into two parts, we will return demonstrate two learned harness combinations with our partners. Feedback will be provided by the instructor as participants employ all the knowledge and tools we’ve learned to bring our partners into the air for a completely new suspension experience.

Skill Level : Basic knowledge of suspension safety, how to lock off uplines. IF this is your first suspension, we ask that you keep it to partials, and take another class focused on suspension safety.

Prerequisite : Knowledge & experience with suspension.

Materials : one 6ft bamboo pole. Bamboo 101 class, where we will be going over how to tie a clove hitch, and two bamboo lashing techniques (we will not be breaking these items down again in the 201-levelclass). Knowledge of an arms-free chest harness and a hip harness will be helpful for the completion of this class, but not required.
