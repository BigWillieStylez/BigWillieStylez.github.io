Rope Fundamentals

Instructor : MedusaM00n

This class will cover the fundamentals of rope. The experience you need can range since we are going over some basics. You can be new but having some rope knowledge would be preferred since we will discuss things like uplines and some more experienced techniques. The plan for this class is to go over safety,  nerve placement, single columns, double columns, a simple Futomomo tie, how to handle your rope for the best consistent tension throughout, why you are doing rope, what do you wish you achieve and the various different types of rope. Its possible we might do a little upline management and talk about some self tying tips for anyone who’s interested.

Skill Level : No experience needed

Prerequisite : None

Materials : 4-5 hanks of rope

Dancing With Myself

self-tying

Instructor : Miss Doctor

Sometimes it’s hard to find a rope partner. And sometimes you just want to spend some quality alone time... with rope. Self-tying has been a critical part of my exploration of rope - as both a top and a bottom. Tying yourself is an opportunity to explore tension, lab out new ties, work on rope handling, create beautiful art, and even enjoy some torturous ropey goodness! Tying Different Bodies

Instructor : Miss Doctor

Much of the rope bondage that we learn is geared towards tying a particular “type” of body. Chest harnesses and TK's are built around breasts. Many lift techniques for suspension presuppose a rigger that is larger than the bottom and presuppose a more petite bottom. Hip harnesses are designed for narrow waists and wider hips and particular types of genitals. People with different body shapes and sizes like getting tied up too, and often smaller-bodied people often enjoy tying them. This class is geared toward understanding the challenges involved in less “traditional” tying dynamics, with an emphasis on how to manage mechanical challenges that may arise. We will discuss ways to alter tying and suspension techniques to adapt to different sizes and shapes and how to prevent injuries in rigger and bottom alike.

Skill Level : Pertinent to all levels, but intermediate if you plan to suspend.

Prerequisite : Bottoms must have experience being suspended. Riggers should have either a chest harness or gote that they know works well for their bottom. Riggers should have experience doing suspension.

Materials : 8-10 hanks of rope, 5+ carabiners (NECESSARY!)

Agura

Instructor : Isean

The agura shibari, or lotus leg tie, is something which is highly versatile and can be modified to be very supportive or very sadistic. We will delve into construction of a solid agura pattern, how to load it, and explore a sequences in the air. Agura allows for unconventional twists on traditional poses, and those are what we'll be exploring. Students will leave well versed in both the tying of the harness, and what they can do with it.

Skill Level : Intermediate, advance hands on

Prerequisite : Have a solid TK or chest harness & up lines

Materials : Full rope kit & suspension gear

Oh Sh!t Part 1 & Part 2

Instructor : St3amPunk

When Shit Goes Wrong in Rope is a 2 part class that reviews common injuries sustained in both suspension and floor rope and the appropriate responses to them. This includes everything from cumulative muscle, joint, and nerve injuries to medical emergencies. 

This class does not take the place of Certified CPR and First Aid instruction through accredited agencies and those certifications are highly recommended, especially for those who tie.

This class is recommended for everyone from novice to expert level of rigging or bottoming. There will be activities and information appropriate for all levels. This is a great class for partners! Participants who want to tie or be tied for activities should bring their rope kits and Bottom Bags.

Skill Level : No experience needed

Prerequisite : None

Materials : Interested in tying or be tied, rope kit or bottom bag

Intermediate Rope Bottoming: Rope Challenges

Instructor : IPCookieMonster

This class will begin with some basic pain management strategies, with a focus on breathing. We will practice timing ourselves in several moderately stressful positions to learn how to predict & manage ourselves in those positions. Afterwards, we will work on developing more sophisticated communication skills for intermediate suspension bottoms. This will primarily be accomplished through an exercise we'll call the Massive Over Communication Challenge in which bottoms basically give their tops a stream-of-consciousness account of everything that is happening sensation-wise as they are being tied, & in which they practice estimating how long they have "left" in various positions. You do not need to bring a rigger to this class, but they are very welcome! 

Skill Level : Intermediate

Prerequisite : Basic suspension bottoming knowledge for safety, negotiation, & body care

Materials : May bring a rigger

Bridging the Communication Chasm Between Tops & bottoms

Instructor : IPCookieMonster

Riggers usually say that the most valuable skill for a rope bottom is good communication. However, there are serious challenges to becoming a skilled communicator as a rope bottom. These include subcultural shame around “topping from the bottom” & the fact that almost all of the vocabulary of bondage was created by riggers to talk about their priorities—which are frequently not the priorities of rope bottoms. In this workshop, we’ll work together to look for better strategies to help rope tops & bottoms communicate with one another to create mutually satisfying experiences.

Skill Level : All levels

Prerequisite : None

Materials : None

Tiny Top Tips

Instructor : Ropeosaur

Are you a smaller rigger? Or a Bottom who wants to pursue a rope relationship with someone smaller to you? Let’s discuss ways to navigate these relationships with the intention of physical & emotional safety. Join Ropeosaur, a petite AFAB rigger, & WrappedInString, a taller, curvy rope Bottom, as we discuss: Healthy negotiations when there’s notable size differences, tips & tricks for suspension with bigger Bottoms, how to use mechanical advantages, tools to keep in your rope bag, and strengthening exercises that directly impact rope performance. Once you master these skills, you’ll be well on your way to safely suspending nearly any BODY in rope, while respecting both top’s & bottom’s physical & emotional boundaries. Please join us as we help you navigate getting people safely into the air as we support larger bodies in rope, and/or the smaller riggers who tie them.

Skill Level : Intermediate to Advanced

Prerequisite : Knowledge & experience with suspension.

Materials : A tying partner, rope, & any materials needed for suspension.
